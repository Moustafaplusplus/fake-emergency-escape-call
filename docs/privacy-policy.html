About Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 21, 2026

Fake Emergency Escape Call (“the App”) is published by the developer of this project. This policy explains how the App handles information. The App is designed for scheduled simulated calls only — it does not place real phone calls or contact emergency services.

Summary

  • We do not collect, sell, or share your personal data with servers or third-party analytics in version 1.0.
  • All scheduled calls, messages, templates, and settings stay on your device.
  • No account or sign-in is required.

Information stored on your device

The App may store the following locally on your phone:

  • Caller names and messages you enter for simulated calls
  • Schedule times and call status (scheduled, completed, declined, etc.)
  • Message templates and app preferences (speech rate, vibration defaults, onboarding status)
  • Optional caller photos if you choose to add them in a future version

This data is saved using Android local storage (Room database and DataStore). It is not transmitted to our servers because the App has no backend in v1.

Information we do not collect

  • Name, email, or phone number for an account (no accounts)
  • Precise location
  • Contacts from your address book (unless you type a name manually)
  • Analytics, advertising IDs, or crash reporting SDK data in v1

Android permissions

The App may request permissions to provide its features. They are used only on-device:

  • Notifications — show ringing and call alerts when the App is in the background
  • Exact alarms — trigger scheduled simulated calls at the time you choose
  • Full-screen intent — display the incoming-call screen over the lock screen when allowed by your device
  • Vibrate — vibration while a simulated call is ringing
  • Foreground service (media playback) — play ringtone reliably while ringing
  • Wake lock — keep the screen on during an active simulated call session
  • Receive boot completed — reschedule alarms after device restart where supported

The App does not use the microphone to record you in v1. Text-to-speech plays a script you wrote after you answer a simulated call.

Third-party services

Version 1.0 does not integrate third-party analytics, advertising, or cloud backup SDKs. Text-to-speech uses the speech engine installed on your device (for example Google Text-to-speech), which is governed by that provider’s policies.

Children’s privacy

The App is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Data retention and deletion

Data remains on your device until you delete it in the App, clear app storage in system settings, or uninstall the App. Uninstalling removes local app data.

Security

Data is stored in the App’s private storage area on your device, protected by Android’s application sandbox. No method of local storage is 100% secure; keep your device locked and updated.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when the App changes (for example if analytics or cloud features are added). The “Last updated” date at the top will change. Continued use after updates means you accept the revised policy.

Contact

For privacy questions or requests, open an issue on the project repository: GitHub Issues.
